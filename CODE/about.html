ABOUT THE VELOCIRAPTOR

In order to properly comprehend the threat of a velociraptor, one must first understand the workings of the velociraptor itself. Here are some facts about and skills of the velociraptor.

Basic Facts

Velociraptor is the genus name of several species of dromaeosaurid dinosaur, the type of dinosaur that is most closely related to birds. Velociraptors are approximately 80 cm tall, and 2 m long, including tail. They usually weigh around 15 to 17 kg, although exceptional individuals have grown up to 35 kg.

Being entirely feathered, velociraptors have the overall impression of a very strange-looking bird. Flocks of velociraptors have been mistaken for wild turkeys from afar, much to the dismay of the human watching them.

Skills

Velociraptors have three deeply curved claws on each of their forelimbs, and one three-inch-long claw on its hind foot. While velociraptor claws are not sharp enough to fully disembowel a human, they are sufficient for tearing open vital organs in the throat. The velociraptors know this, and the throat is where they will attack first if hunting.

Their top speed is approximately 60 kph, or 37 mph, although they do not have much stamina and, similar to a cheetah, will exhaust their energy within a few minutes. If you have a good head start, you may be able to outrun a velociraptor, although if you are close by you will not make it. Velociraptors can also jump up to 10 feet vertically. Your second story windows are not safe.
